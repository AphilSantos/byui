A Jester’s Tale

By Riley Gilmore

Jinx sat huddled in a dark corner of a cold, dank dungeon, imprisoned in the most feared bowels of Malmoral Castle for what now seemed like eons. Her black clothes, caked in sweat and dirt, hung loosely on her frail body. Her long, raven black hair fell in greasy strands along the length of her back and cascaded over her face, blessedly obscuring the dismal view of her surroundings. Her mesmerizing gray eyes now glistened with ever-present tears, where once upon a time they sparkled with joy.

She had no real sense of time anymore, but somehow instinctively knew that the sun would be up soon and would cast shards of light through the cracks in the wall near the ceiling. She lived to see that small glimpse of the outside world, cursing cloudy days that denied her that one small pleasure. Sadly, the arrival of the dawn also brought with it the certainty that he would soon appear, as he had done every morning since her capture.

Her reverie was abruptly interrupted by the sound of heavy footsteps coming closer and closer. Her captor, Captain of the Guard Jean LaSalle, was approaching as expected. He knocked three times on the massive wooden dungeon door, not out of any kind of courtesy or respect for Jinx, but as a way of frightening the various rats with which she shared her living quarters into scattering away in a frenzy. The door slowly opened on squeaky hinges, LaSalle no longer even bothering to lock it after himself upon leaving her each day as Jinx hadn’t had the energy to stand, let alone attempt an escape, for some months now.

Eager eyes held wide, Jinx slowly raised her face to meet the meager rays of sunshine as they fought to enlighten her cell, basking her in images of days long gone when she was free, loved, and happy. She taunted herself with memories of delicious meals shared with family, lively jousting tournaments, the handsome smile of the man she loved, and the cherished friends she would die to protect…
